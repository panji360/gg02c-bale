Information: Bale-Bale


Often found in the same walled groups of the houses, the “bale-bale” was a building with an open structure.


Standing on “umpak” (stone foundations to support wooden poles), its roof was also decorated with curly corners (“ukel”).


“Bale-bale” was used as a public space for communal activities. Like Majapahit houses, the architecture of these buildings can also still be seen today in traditional Bali villages.

Bale-Bale Contents


"Wajik" is one of the traditional snacks known to have existed since Majapahit era (14th century or even earlier). It is still popular today in Java (Indonesia) and Brunei.


Image from model by Stephanie Budiman


"Kendang" is a drum of cylindrical shape tapering slightly at one end. In Balinese Gamelan (traditional music), kendang has a body made of jackfruit wood and heads made of cowhide. It is played in pairs, consisting of kendang "wadon" (female drum), which has lower pitch, and kendang "lanang" (male drum), which is more high-pitched.
